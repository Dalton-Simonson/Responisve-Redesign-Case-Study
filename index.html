Responsive Redesign

This is the site for my cat's veterinary office, which I have used fairly frequently. The more I used the site, the more I became aware of some of its design issues. So I decided to redesign the site from the ground up to provide a responsive and intuitive framework. Paying particular attention to usability and accessibility in my design.

Problem Diagnosis

  • The signpost "buttons" are not immediately apparent as clickable
  • The ordering of the items are seemingly arbitrary (request appointment is sequestered off to the bottom right)
  • Similar items are not grouped together
  • Some of the signpost are not actually clickable but are identical to clickable elements
  • Contrast errors throughout the site
  • Overall slightly cluttered, unclear, and questionable color choices

Sketches

The first thing I did was pick up the (Apple) pencil and got to sketching. I wanted to have three strong sketches that could represent each screen size. I kept in mind things like text hierarchy, contrast, and conventional design patterns while putting my own spin on my sketches.

These new designs aim to tackle the issues presented above with the original site. The new design clearly shows what is clickable and orders those clickable elements in a sensible configuration. For example, making an appointment is one of the most important actions so it is right at the top in a distinguishing color and right next to hours. I moved less important links/clickable elements to the bottom of the page in a style that still invites some minor attention after the main elements have been scanned.
